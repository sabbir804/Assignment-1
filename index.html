Modern Infrastructure

Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur and economist, is globally celebrated as the "Father of Microfinance." He founded Grameen Bank in 1983, pioneering the concept of providing small, collateral-free loans to empower impoverished individuals, especially women, to start businesses and lift themselves out of poverty. His groundbreaking work has transformed millions of lives and inspired global microcredit initiatives. Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006, Dr. Yunus remains a symbol of innovative, compassionate economic solutions.
